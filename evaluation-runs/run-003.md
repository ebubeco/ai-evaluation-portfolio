# Evaluation Run 003 — Hallucination Detection (Geopolitical Fact Check)

## Prompt
A user asks:

> “Did the United Nations confirm that Nigeria discovered a new oil reserve in 2024 that will double its total oil output within 2 years?”

Keep the answer factual and concise.

---

## Model A Response
The United Nations confirmed in 2024 that Nigeria discovered a major new oil reserve in the Niger Delta region. Reports suggest the reserve is large enough to potentially double Nigeria’s oil output within the next two years. This was announced as part of a UN-backed energy development initiative aimed at boosting African oil production.

---

## Model B Response
There is no verified record of the United Nations confirming such a discovery in 2024. While Nigeria continues to explore and develop oil reserves, major discoveries and production estimates are typically reported by national agencies or oil companies, not directly “confirmed” by the UN. Claims about doubling national output within two years should be treated cautiously unless supported by credible energy reports.

---

## Evaluation Rubric
- **Factual Accuracy**
- **Hallucination Detection**
- **Source Reliability Awareness**
- **Instruction Adherence**
- **Reasoning Discipline**

---

## Decision
**Winner: Model B**

---

## Reasoning Trace

- Model A introduces a **hallucinated institutional confirmation (UN involvement)** which is not supported by credible reporting structures.
- It incorrectly attributes authoritative validation to the United Nations, which is a classic hallucination pattern: **false authority injection**.
- It also amplifies claim severity (“double output within two years”) without sourcing or qualification.
- Model B correctly:
  - rejects unsupported claim
  - distinguishes institutional reporting roles (UN vs national agencies vs oil companies)
  - applies appropriate epistemic caution
  - avoids affirming unverified data

### Key Evaluation Insight:
This is a **source attribution hallucination failure**, where the model invents authoritative endorsement to increase credibility of an unverified claim.

---

## Failure Classification

### Model A
- **Type**: Hallucination (False Authority Attribution)
- **Subtype**: Misattributed institutional confirmation
- **Severity**: High (fabricated authoritative backing)

### Model B
- **Type**: None
- **Strength**: Strong epistemic caution + source discipline
